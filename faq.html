FAQ

This is a list of frequently asked questions (FAQ) for Hawkmatix projects. This FAQ is intended to supplement, not replace, the Hawkmatix project manuals.

What is Hawkmatix?

Hawkmatix is a hub for financial analysis and trading software development. They publish projects and reports that cover a wide range of topics including trading, finance, mathematics, and computer science. Most projects and reports approach problems from a mathematical standpoint and include derivations of principles and numerical solutions.

Who uses Hawkmatix?

Mainly traders and financial analysts use Hawkmatix projects and reports, but they are also commonly referenced by mathematicians and others in academia.

Who develops Hawkmatix?

Hawkmatix projects come about from research and development done by a variety of different people. The original projects and software that would eventually become Hawkmatix started in about 2006 with Andrew C. Hawkins at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University.

Why Hawkmatix?

Hawkmatix spawned from the need for open source and freely available research, projects, and reports in the trading and finance industries.

Are Hawkmatix projects developed for any platform?

Commonly, projects will include software associated with a particular trading platform. As of now, NinjaTrader is the only supported platform. In the near future additional platforms shall be added.

How are Hawkmatix projects licensed?

Projects are open source and available to anyone under the GNU Lesser General Public License, reports and other site content are covered under the GNU Free Documentation License.

How can I obtain a Hawkmatix project?

Source code for projects is available on the specific project’s page, where you are sure to get the latest version. Since projects are distributed under the terms of the GNU Lesser General Public License, you can get projects from a friend who has a copy, or from the Hawkmatix website. Depending on the platform the project is developed for, pre-compiled binary packages may be available on the specific project’s page.
